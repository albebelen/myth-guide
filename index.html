Myth Guide

Because gods are messy and Zeus had way too many lovers.

The Twelve Olympians

Zeus

King of the gods; god of the sky and lightning.

The youngest child of the Titans Cronus and Rhea. Brother and husband of Hera and brother of Poseidon, Hades, Demeter, and Hestia

Hera

Queen of the gods; goddess of marriage, women and family.

Sister and wife of Zeus. Being the goddess of marriage, she frequently tried to get revenge on Zeus' lovers and their children.

Poseidon

Gods of the seas.

The middle son of Cronus and Rhea. Married to the Nereid Amphitrite; although, as with many of the male Greek gods, he had many lovers.

Demeter

Goddess of harvest, fertility and agriculture.

The middle daughter of Cronus and Rhea. The mother of Persephone, Despoine and Arion.

Athena

Goddess of wisdom, handicraft and warfare.

The daughter of Zeus and Metis, she rose from her father's head fully grown and in full battle armor.

Apollo

God of light, the Sun, poetry, arts and medicine.

The son of Zeus and Leto, and the twin brother of Artemis.

Artemis

Goddess of the hunt, the wilderness, the Moon and archery.

The twin sister of Apollo.

Ares

God of war, violence and bloodshed.

The son of Zeus and Hera. Aphrodite's lover.

Aphrodite

Goddess of love, passion and beauty.

The daughter of Zeus and Dione, or born from the sea foam after Uranus' blood shed into the sea. Married to Haephaestus.

Haephaestus

God of the forge, craftsmanship and invention; master blacksmith and craftsman of the gods.

The son of Hera, either by Zeus or through parthenogenesis.

Hermes

God of travel, commerce and thieves; messenger of the gods.

The son of Zeus and the nypmh Maia.

Hestia

Goddess of the hearth and fire.

She is the first child of Cronus and Rhea.

Some list her as one of the Olympians in favor of Dionysus.
